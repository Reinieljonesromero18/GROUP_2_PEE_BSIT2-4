Effects of Biodiversity Loss

2024-02-01•7 min read•By The Digital Green Journal

Understanding the consequences of declining species and ecosystem health.

Biodiversity loss is one of the most critical environmental challenges...

What is Biodiversity Loss?

Biodiversity loss refers to the declining variety of life forms in the biosphere, including species extinction and ecosystem degradation.

Causes

Habitat destruction, pollution, climate change, and overexploitation of resources.

Examples:

  • Habitat fragmentation
  • Pollution impacts
  • Overfishing

Ecological Consequences

Loss of biodiversity disrupts ecosystem services that humans depend on for survival.

Ecosystem Collapse

When key species disappear, entire ecosystems can fail.

Examples:

  • Pollinator decline
  • Food chain disruption
  • Soil degradation

Services We Lose

Clean water, food production, climate regulation, disease control.

Examples:

  • Water filtration
  • Crop pollination
  • Air purification
