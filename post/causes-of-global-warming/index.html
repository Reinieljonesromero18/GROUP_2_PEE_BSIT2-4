What is Global Warming?

Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature. This phenomenon is primarily driven by human activities that result in the accumulation of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.

Definition

The progressive warming of the planet due to increased greenhouse gas concentrations.

Examples:

  • Ice caps melting
  • Sea levels rising
  • More extreme weather events

Primary Causes

Several human activities contribute significantly to global warming, with fossil fuel burning being the largest contributor.

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

The burning of fossil fuels for energy releases CO2, methane, and nitrous oxide into the atmosphere.

Examples:

  • Coal power plants
  • Vehicle emissions
  • Industrial processes

Deforestation

Forests act as carbon sinks. When cleared, they release stored carbon.

Examples:

  • Agricultural expansion
  • Logging operations
  • Urban development
